How to Reach ₹1 Crore with SIP —
A Realistic Plan for Every Income Level

📅 April 2026⏱ 7 min read✍ Black Belt Code Labs
Advertisement

₹1 Crore is the most searched financial goal in India. It sounds enormous — but with SIP, it's far more achievable than most people think. The math is clear, the path is straightforward. This guide shows you exactly what it takes at every income level.

The Numbers First: Monthly SIP for ₹1 Crore

Monthly SIP needed to reach ₹1 Crore (at 12% returns)
In 10 years₹43,700/month
In 15 years₹19,800/month
In 20 years₹10,000/month
In 25 years₹5,320/month
In 30 years₹2,880/month

The most striking number here: ₹10,000/month for 20 years crosses ₹1 Crore. That's a SIP amount accessible to anyone earning ₹40,000+ per month. The secret isn't the amount — it's the time.

Why Starting Early Changes Everything

Every 5 years of delay roughly doubles the required monthly SIP. Someone who starts at 25 needs ₹5,320/month to reach ₹1 Crore by 50. Someone who starts at 35 needs ₹19,800/month for the same goal at 50. The person who starts 10 years late needs to invest 4 times more per month — that's the compounding penalty for delay.

Advertisement

This is why the single most powerful action you can take right now, regardless of your income, is to start a SIP today — even a small one.

Plan by Income Level

₹25,000–₹40,000 Monthly Income

Start with ₹3,000–₹5,000/month SIP. You won't reach ₹1 Crore in 10 years at this level, but over 20–25 years, ₹5,000/month at 12% becomes ₹49.9 lakhs. Add a 10% annual step-up and it crosses ₹1.12 Crore in 20 years. The step-up is your superpower at lower income levels.

₹50,000–₹80,000 Monthly Income

₹10,000–₹15,000/month SIP is achievable. At ₹10,000/month for 20 years at 12%, you reach exactly ₹1 Crore. Add a 10% annual step-up and you hit ₹2 Crore+ in the same period. This is the sweet spot for most middle-income Indian families.

₹1 Lakh+ Monthly Income

₹25,000–₹50,000/month SIP. At ₹25,000/month for 15 years at 12%, corpus is ₹1.25 Crore. For 20 years, it becomes ₹2.49 Crore. At this income level, the goal shifts from "reaching ₹1 Crore" to "financial independence."

The Step-Up Strategy: Most Powerful Tool You're Ignoring

Most Indian salaried employees get 8–12% annual salary hikes. If you increase your SIP by the same percentage each year, the impact is dramatic:

₹10,000/month SIP — 20 years at 12%
Without step-up₹99.9 Lakhs (~₹1 Crore)
With 5% annual step-up₹1.39 Crore
With 10% annual step-up₹1.99 Crore
With 15% annual step-up₹2.91 Crore

A 10% step-up doubles your final corpus without requiring you to "find" extra money — it simply channels your income growth into investments.

Which Funds Should You Use for This Goal?

For a ₹1 Crore goal over 10–20 years, a diversified equity portfolio works best:

  • 60% Nifty 50 Index Fund — Low cost, broad market exposure, historically reliable
  • 25% Flexi-cap Fund — Active management across market caps for potential alpha
  • 15% Mid-cap Index Fund — Higher growth potential, acceptable volatility for long horizons

If you're investing for 80C benefit simultaneously, replace the flexi-cap allocation with an ELSS fund — same equity exposure, same returns, plus tax deduction.

The Most Common Mistake: Waiting for the "Right" Time

Markets will always seem either too high (risky to invest) or too low (scary to invest). SIP eliminates the timing problem — you invest regardless of market levels. Every market correction is an opportunity for your SIP to buy more units at lower prices.

The right time to start is always today. Not after the next election. Not after the budget. Not after markets correct. Today.
