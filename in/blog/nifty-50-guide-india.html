What is Nifty 50?
Complete Guide for Indian Investors

The Nifty 50 is an index that tracks the stock prices of India's 50 largest publicly listed companies. It is maintained by NSE (National Stock Exchange of India) and represents approximately 65% of the total value of all stocks listed on NSE. When news says "the market fell 2% today," they mean the Nifty 50 fell 2%.

What Exactly Is an Index?

An index is a measuring tool. Just as the temperature tells you how hot the weather is, the Nifty 50 tells you how the Indian stock market is performing. It does not mean anything is literally being bought or sold — it is a number that represents the collective value of 50 companies at any given moment.

When you hear "Nifty at 22,500," it means that the weighted combined stock price of India's top 50 companies equals 22,500 points today. When the index goes from 22,000 to 22,500, it means those 50 companies collectively became 2.27% more valuable.

Nifty 50 vs Sensex — What Is the Difference?

Both are Indian stock market indices, but they differ in scope:

  • Sensex — tracks 30 companies, maintained by BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange). India's oldest index (since 1979).
  • Nifty 50 — tracks 50 companies, maintained by NSE (National Stock Exchange). More diversified, more widely used by fund managers.
  • Both move in the same direction almost always — they are both measuring the same underlying economy.

For investing purposes, both are similar. Nifty 50 is more commonly used for index funds because NSE has higher trading volume and most new-age index products track Nifty 50.

How to Invest in Nifty 50

You cannot buy the Nifty 50 index directly. You invest in it through a Nifty 50 Index Fund — a mutual fund that automatically holds all 50 stocks in the same proportion as the index.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Nifty 50 at 22,500 mean?+
It means the combined weighted value of India's top 50 companies equals 22,500 index points today. The Nifty 50 started at 1,000 points in November 1995. At 22,500, it has grown 22.5x in 29 years — a CAGR of approximately 11.4% excluding dividends.
Can Nifty 50 go to zero?+
For Nifty 50 to go to zero, all 50 of India's largest companies would need to become completely worthless simultaneously — which has never happened to any major economy's stock index in history. Individual companies can fail, but the index constantly replaces failing companies with stronger ones. The Nifty 50 has recovered from every single crash in its history.
Is Nifty 50 index fund better than fixed deposit?+
For money you will not need for 7+ years, yes. Nifty 50 has delivered 12–14% CAGR over 20 years vs FD's 6.5–7.5%. After accounting for 30% tax on FD interest, the post-tax FD return is approximately 5.1–5.25% — far below inflation. Nifty 50 index fund LTCG is 12.5% only on gains above ₹1.25 lakh/year. For money needed within 3 years, FD or liquid funds are safer.
