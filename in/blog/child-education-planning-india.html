Child Education Planning in India —
Costs, Tools, and the Exact Savings Plan

📅 May 2026⏱ 8 min read✍ Black Belt Code Labs
Advertisement

Education is the second most expensive goal for most Indian families after retirement. And unlike retirement, you can't delay it — the college admission date is fixed. The earlier you plan, the less you need to save per month. Here's everything you need to know.

The Education Inflation Problem

Education costs in India have been rising at 10–12% annually — far faster than general inflation (6%). At 10% education inflation, a ₹15 lakh engineering degree today becomes ₹38 lakhs in 10 years and ₹98 lakhs in 15 years. If you're planning for a child born today, the cost at their graduation is 3–5 times today's cost.

This is why you must plan with inflation-adjusted future costs — not current fees.

Best Instruments for Child Education Fund

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (For Girls)

If your child is a girl below 10 years, SSY is one of the best instruments available. Current rate: 8.2% p.a., fully tax-free (EEE). Maximum ₹1.5 lakh/year (qualifies for 80C). 50% can be withdrawn at age 18 for education. This should be the safe/debt component of any girl child's education fund.

Child Education Insurance Plans — Avoid

Traditional child ULIPs and endowment policies are poor value. High charges, opaque structures, low returns (5–6%). You're better off with term insurance (for protection) + mutual fund SIP (for returns) separately — called "Buy Term, Invest the Rest."

Equity Mutual Funds (Core of Education Fund)

For goals 7+ years away: diversified equity SIP in index funds or flexi-cap funds. This is where the bulk of education corpus should be built — higher returns to beat education inflation.

Debt Funds (As Goal Approaches)

3–5 years before the education goal, gradually shift from equity to debt funds. This protects the corpus from market volatility when you need the money soon.

A Practical Plan: Child Born Today, Engineering in 18 Years

Target: ₹50 lakhs (current cost) × 1.10^18 (10% education inflation) = ₹2.8 Crore needed at child's college age.

Monthly SIP required at 12% returns starting today: approximately ₹27,400/month.

Alternatively: ₹15,000/month SIP + 10% annual step-up for 18 years at 12% = approximately ₹2.6 Crore. Far more affordable.

This is why step-up SIP combined with starting early is the most practical approach for education planning.

Don't Forget Education Loans as Backup

You don't need to fund 100% of education from savings. Education loans (at 8.5–11%) come with:

  • Section 80E deduction on interest (no upper limit, 8 years)
  • No repayment during study period (moratorium)
  • Builds financial responsibility in the child

A practical strategy: fund 60–70% through SIP, let the child fund the rest with an education loan. This dramatically reduces the monthly savings required while the loan interest deduction improves the cost.
