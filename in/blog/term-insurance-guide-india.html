Term Insurance Guide India 2026 – How Much Cover Do You Need?

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 11 min read ✍ CalcPhi Editorial Team
⚠️ This article is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice. Consult a SEBI-registered advisor before making investment decisions.

Term insurance is the simplest, cheapest, and most important financial product for any Indian with financial dependants. A ₹1 crore term plan at 30 costs less than a Netflix subscription. Yet most Indians either don't have one, bought the wrong type, or bought too little. This guide fixes all of that.

Why Pure Term Insurance, Not Endowment or ULIP

Insurance agents make 25–35% commission on endowment and ULIP products, versus 2–5% on term plans. This explains why most agents push everything except term. The data is unambiguous:

The endowment plan bundles insurance and investment into one product — doing both badly. Buy term for protection, invest the rest in mutual funds. This is not a controversial opinion among financial experts.

How Much Cover Do You Need?

Use the DIME method (Debt + Income + Mortgage + Education):

  • Debt: Total outstanding loans (home loan + personal loan + car loan)
  • Income replacement: Annual income × remaining working years (minimum 10x annual income)
  • Mortgage: Outstanding home loan (if not counted above)
  • Education: Children's education fund (college + professional degree cost)

Riders Worth Adding (and Which to Skip)

Worth adding:

  • Critical Illness Rider: Pays lump sum on diagnosis of 40+ critical illnesses. Useful if you'd want to stop working for treatment. Cost: ₹200–400/month extra for ₹25L cover.
  • Accidental Death Benefit: Doubles payout if death is accidental. Very cheap — ₹100–200/month for ₹1 Crore extra cover.
  • Waiver of Premium on Disability: If you become disabled, future premiums are waived while coverage continues. Almost always worth the small cost.

Skip these: Return of Premium rider (adds 50–80% to cost for essentially 4–5% return), Accidental Total Disability (complex definitions often lead to claim rejections).

5 Mistakes That Lead to Claim Rejection

  1. Non-disclosure of medical history. Never hide pre-existing conditions. The insurer investigates at claim time — hiding information is the #1 cause of rejection. Declare everything: diabetes, hypertension, smoking, past surgeries.
  2. Incorrect nominee details. Ensure nominee name matches Aadhaar/PAN exactly. Update nominee after marriage, divorce, or death of nominee.
  3. Letting policy lapse. Set ECS auto-debit for premium payment. A 30-day grace period exists, but after that the policy lapses and no claim is payable.
  4. Suicide exclusion (first year). Most policies exclude suicide in the first 12 months.
  5. Occupational hazard non-disclosure. If you work in mining, construction, or high-risk occupations, declare this. Undisclosed occupational risk leads to rejection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much term insurance do I need in India?+
Use the rule: minimum 10x annual income. For a ₹12 lakh/year earner, minimum ₹1.2 crore cover. Better formula: Annual income × 15 + Outstanding loans + Children's education fund. Review and increase cover after every major life event — marriage, child birth, home purchase.
Is LIC term plan better than private?+
LIC has the highest claim settlement ratio (98.7%) and government backing — these are genuine advantages. Private insurers like Max Life (99.3%), Tata AIA, and HDFC Life also have excellent CSR and lower premiums. For maximum peace of mind: split coverage between LIC and one private insurer (two separate policies of ₹50L each).
At what age should I buy term insurance?+
The earlier the better — premiums lock in at current age and health status. Ideal: 25–30 years. Latest: before 35. After 40, premiums increase significantly. Buy immediately if you have dependants regardless of age. Some conditions become uninsurable with age — buy before health issues emerge.
