NSC Calculator — National Savings Certificate 2026

National Savings Certificate (NSC) is a fixed-income scheme offered by India Post, backed by the Government of India. It offers a guaranteed return of 7.7% per annum for FY 2025-26, compounded annually, with a fixed 5-year maturity. The interest is reinvested automatically and paid at maturity — there is no annual interest payout like FD.

Maturity Formula: A = P × (1 + 7.7/100)5 where P is the invested amount. On ₹1 lakh: maturity = ₹1,45,149 — a gain of ₹45,149 over 5 years.

NSC vs PPF vs FD — Which is Best?

NSC at 7.7%: Interest is taxable but reinvested interest qualifies for 80C deduction each year (except the last year). Effective post-tax return at 30% slab ≈ 5.4%. PPF at 7.1%: Fully tax-free. Post-tax return = 7.1% — better than NSC for 30% slab investors despite the lower headline rate. FD at 7.5%: Interest taxable annually. Post-tax return at 30% slab ≈ 5.25%. NSC beats FD slightly because interest is reinvested and tax is deferred.

Tax Benefit on NSC

NSC investment qualifies for deduction under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh limit). The accrued interest for years 1 through 4 is treated as fresh investment and also qualifies for 80C deduction — effectively giving you 5 years of 80C benefit from one purchase. Only year 5 interest is taxable as income with no offsetting 80C benefit.
