Gold Investment — Key Questions

Should I invest in SGB, Gold ETF, or physical gold?+
For most investors, SGB is the best choice: it gives gold price returns PLUS 2.5% annual interest, has no storage cost, no purity risk, and if held to 8-year maturity, gains are completely tax-free. Gold ETF is better if you need high liquidity. Physical gold only makes sense for jewellery with emotional value — as an investment, it's the worst option due to making charges and storage costs.
What has been gold's CAGR in India over 20 years?+
Gold has delivered approximately 11-12% CAGR in India over the past 20 years (2004-2024), outperforming FD and beating inflation significantly. However, gold returns are highly lumpy — it can be flat for 3-5 years and then spike 30-40% in a single year. This makes gold an excellent portfolio diversifier but a poor primary wealth creator compared to equity over long periods.
How much of my portfolio should be in gold?+
Most financial planners recommend 5-15% of your portfolio in gold as a hedge against inflation, currency depreciation, and geopolitical risk. Gold and equity have low correlation — gold often rises when equity falls — making it a valuable diversifier. Avoid more than 15-20% in gold as it generates no cash flow and has lower long-term returns than equity.
