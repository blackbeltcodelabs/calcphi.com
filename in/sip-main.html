SIP Calculator — Everything You Need to Know

What is a SIP and how does it work?+
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund every month automatically. It uses rupee cost averaging — buying more units when markets fall and fewer when they rise. Over 10+ years, SIP in equity funds has historically delivered 10–15% annualized returns in India.
How is SIP return calculated?+
SIP uses the future value of annuity formula: FV = P × [(1 + r)ⁿ – 1] / r × (1 + r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly rate (annual ÷ 12), and n is total months. This calculator applies exact monthly compounding.
What is a Step-Up SIP?+
A Step-Up SIP automatically increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage every year. Starting at ₹10,000 with 10% step-up means ₹11,000 next year, ₹12,100 the year after. This aligns with natural income growth and dramatically improves your final corpus.
What is a realistic SIP return rate?+
For large-cap equity funds, 10–12% p.a. is a realistic long-term benchmark. Mid/small-cap funds may return 13–15% with higher volatility. Debt funds return 6–8%. The default 12% represents a conservative historical average for diversified equity funds in India.
How much should I invest in SIP per month?+
Invest at least 20% of your monthly income. Use our Target SIP Calculator to work backwards from your goal — enter your target corpus, timeline, and expected return to find the exact monthly SIP needed.

Frequently Asked Clarifications

Before using any financial calculator, it helps to understand a few fundamentals that apply across all types of calculations.

Gross vs Net Inputs

Always confirm whether the calculator expects gross income (before tax) or net income (take-home pay). For India income tax calculators, enter your total annual gross salary. For EMI affordability calculations, use your net monthly take-home. For investment calculators, use the actual amount you plan to invest after taxes and essential expenses. Entering the wrong figure significantly distorts the output.

Annual vs Monthly Figures

Indian financial products frequently express rates and amounts annually while payments occur monthly. A 12% annual SIP return is approximately 0.95% per month compounded. A 9% annual home loan rate is 0.75% per month on the reducing balance. Always verify whether the rate you enter should be annual or monthly — most calculators expect the annual rate and convert internally.

Inflation Adjustment

Long-term projections for retirement corpus, target SIP amounts, or home loan affordability should be viewed in today's rupee value. With India's historical inflation averaging 5-6% per year, Rs 50 lakhs today will have the purchasing power of approximately Rs 30 lakhs after 10 years. For retirement planning, always use real returns (nominal return minus inflation rate) to project meaningful outcomes.
