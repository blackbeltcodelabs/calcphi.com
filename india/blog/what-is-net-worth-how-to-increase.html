What Is Net Worth —
And How to Grow It Every Year

📅 May 2026⏱ 6 min read✍ Black Belt Code Labs
Advertisement

Net worth is the most important number in personal finance — and the one most Indians never calculate. It's the single clearest picture of your financial health. Here's what it is, how to calculate yours right now, and the fastest ways to grow it.

What Is Net Worth?

Net Worth = Total Assets – Total Liabilities

Assets: everything you own that has monetary value — savings accounts, investments, property, vehicles, gold, EPF/PPF balance. Liabilities: everything you owe — home loan, car loan, personal loan, credit card outstanding, family loans.

If your assets total ₹60 lakhs and your liabilities total ₹40 lakhs, your net worth is ₹20 lakhs. Simple.

Why It Matters More Than Income

Income is what you earn. Net worth is what you keep. Two people can earn the same salary and have vastly different net worths based on their spending, savings rate, and liabilities. A ₹50,000/month earner who saves aggressively for 10 years will often have higher net worth than a ₹2 lakh/month earner who spends everything.

Net worth is also what generates passive income in retirement. Your income stops when you stop working. Your net worth (if invested correctly) generates returns indefinitely.

These are guidelines. Your home's equity, inherited assets, and specific circumstances vary. The direction matters more than hitting exact numbers — is your net worth growing year over year?

The 5 Fastest Ways to Increase Net Worth

1. Increase Your Savings Rate

Net worth grows when assets grow faster than liabilities. The simplest lever: save more. Every additional ₹1,000/month saved and invested adds significantly more than ₹1,000 to your net worth over time (through compounding). Going from 10% to 25% savings rate can double your net worth trajectory.

2. Kill High-Interest Debt First

Credit card debt at 36–42% annual interest destroys net worth faster than almost anything. Personal loans at 14–18% are nearly as damaging. Pay these off before investing. Every rupee toward high-interest debt gives a guaranteed 36% return — nothing in the market matches that.

3. Invest Consistently in Equity

The largest component of net worth growth for most people comes from investment returns. ₹20,000/month in equity SIP at 12% for 20 years adds ₹1.99 Crore to your net worth. This single habit, sustained over decades, is the biggest net worth builder available to an ordinary Indian.

4. Grow Your Income

Increasing income with a constant lifestyle is the most powerful net worth accelerator. Every salary hike channelled into investment (not lifestyle) directly compounds. A ₹15,000/month hike invested entirely in SIP adds ₹1.49 Crore in 20 years. Skills, side income, career advancement, or business — growing income creates the raw material for net worth growth.

5. Track It Quarterly

What gets measured gets managed. Calculate your net worth every 3 months. The act of tracking creates awareness and accountability. Most people who start tracking net worth see it improve within 6 months — not because they have more money, but because they start making different decisions with the money they have.

💡 The simplest rule: Every financial decision should pass this test — does it increase or decrease my net worth? EMI on a depreciating car decreases it. SIP increases it. High-interest debt destroys it. Saving increases it.
