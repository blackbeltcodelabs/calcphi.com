The True Cost of Credit Card Debt in America

The average US credit card interest rate in 2026 is approximately 21–22% APR — making credit card debt the most expensive common form of borrowing. A $10,000 balance at 22% APR with minimum payments (2% of balance) takes over 30 years to pay off and costs more than $16,000 in interest alone — nearly doubling the original debt.

Debt Avalanche vs Debt Snowball

Debt Avalanche — pay minimum on all cards, put all extra money on the highest-APR card first. Mathematically optimal — saves the most in total interest. Use this if you are motivated by numbers and can stay disciplined.

Debt Snowball — pay minimum on all cards, put all extra money on the smallest balance first. Provides quick wins and psychological momentum. Multiple studies show this method gets more people debt-free despite being slightly less mathematically optimal.

Balance Transfer Cards — Zero Interest Periods

Many cards offer 0% APR for 12–21 months on balance transfers (with a 3–5% transfer fee). Transferring $8,500 to a 0% card and paying it off in 18 months saves all the interest. The math: 18 months × $472/month = $0 interest vs $2,443 at 22%. The transfer fee ($255–$425) is trivially small compared to the savings. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pay off credit card debt fast?+
The fastest method: pay as much above the minimum as possible each month. Even an extra $50/month dramatically accelerates payoff. Consider balance transfer to 0% APR card, cut expenses temporarily to increase payments, or use a debt consolidation loan at lower rate.
What is the average credit card interest rate in 2026?+
Average credit card APR in the US in 2026 is approximately 21–22%. Some rewards cards charge 24–29%. Store cards often exceed 29%. Credit unions and some online banks offer rates of 12–18% for members with good credit.
Will paying off my credit card hurt my credit score?+
No. Paying off credit card debt helps your credit score by reducing your credit utilization ratio (balance ÷ limit). Keeping utilization below 30% — and ideally below 10% — is a major factor in credit scores. Closing paid-off cards can hurt your score by reducing available credit.
