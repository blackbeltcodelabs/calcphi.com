Australian Take-Home Pay 2025-26

Your take-home pay in Australia depends on your gross salary minus income tax, Medicare Levy, any HELP/HECS repayment, and super contributions. Super is paid on top of your salary by your employer at 11.5% - it is separate from your take-home pay and does not reduce it unless you have a salary sacrifice arrangement.

Take-Home Examples 2025-26

$60,000 gross: Income tax $11,617, Medicare Levy $1,200. Take-home approximately $47,183 per year or $3,932 per month. $100,000 gross: Income tax $24,947, Medicare Levy $2,000. Take-home approximately $73,053 per year or $6,088 per month. $150,000 gross: Income tax $43,447, Medicare Levy $3,000. Take-home approximately $103,553 per year or $8,629 per month.

HECS/HELP Repayment

If you have a HELP debt, compulsory repayments begin at $54,435 of repayment income in 2025-26 at a rate of 1%. Rates increase progressively up to 10% at incomes above $159,664. HELP repayments are deducted by your employer through the tax system. HELP debt is indexed to CPI each year.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions. Australian financial planning rewards consistent superannuation contributions, tax-efficient investment structures, and strategic use of government benefits. Review your super fund performance and fees annually, optimize voluntary contribution strategies based on your marginal tax rate, and consult a licensed financial adviser registered with ASIC for major decisions. Frequently Asked Questions

How much tax do I pay on $80,000 in Australia?+
On $80,000 in 2025-26: Income tax approximately $18,067 (32.5c on income between $45,001 and $80,000 plus base $5,092), Medicare Levy $1,600. Total deductions approximately $19,667. Take-home approximately $60,333 per year or $5,028 per month.
Is super included in my salary in Australia?+
Super is typically paid on top of your stated salary, not out of it. A $100,000 salary plus 11.5% super means your employer pays $115,000 total. However, under Total Remuneration Package arrangements, super may be included in the package total, effectively reducing your cash salary.
When do HECS repayments start?+
Compulsory HECS-HELP repayments begin at $54,435 repayment income in 2025-26. At that income, you repay 1% of your total income. The rate increases progressively to 10% at very high incomes. Repayments are made through the tax system when you lodge your return or through Pay As You Go withholding.
