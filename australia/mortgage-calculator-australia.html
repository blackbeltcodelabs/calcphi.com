Australian Mortgage Basics

Australian home loans use monthly compounding. Most Australian mortgages are variable rate, following the Reserve Bank of Australia cash rate. Fixed rates are available for 1-5 year terms. Most banks allow unlimited extra repayments on variable rate loans, making them highly flexible for accelerating mortgage payoff.

2026 Australian Home Loan Rates

Major bank variable rates (owner-occupier, principal and interest): approximately 6.0 to 6.8%. Fixed 2-year rates: approximately 5.8 to 6.4%. Online lenders and smaller banks often offer 0.3 to 0.7% below major banks. The lowest advertised variable rates from smaller lenders are approximately 5.5% in 2026.

Extra Repayments - The Power of Overpaying

An extra $500 per month on a $600,000 mortgage at 6% over 30 years saves approximately $180,000 in interest and pays off the loan 7 years early. An extra $1,000 per month saves approximately $290,000 and pays off 12 years early. The RBA cash rate affects variable rates, so tracking rate movements is important for budgeting.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average home loan rate in Australia 2026?+
Major bank variable rates for owner-occupiers making principal and interest repayments are approximately 6.0 to 6.8% in 2026. Online lenders offer lower rates around 5.5 to 6.0%. Fixed rates for 2-3 year terms are slightly below variable rates at approximately 5.8 to 6.4%.
How much can I borrow in Australia?+
Australian lenders use the Household Expenditure Measure (HEM) and test serviceability at the loan rate plus 3% buffer. As a rough guide, most lenders will lend approximately 4 to 5 times your gross annual income, subject to your existing debts and expenses. A $150,000 household income may qualify for approximately $600,000 to $750,000 in borrowing.
Is LMI worth paying in Australia?+
Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) applies when your deposit is less than 20% of the purchase price. LMI can cost $10,000 to $30,000 on a $700,000 loan. However, buying sooner and benefiting from property appreciation may outweigh the LMI cost in strong markets. First Home Guarantee allows eligible buyers to purchase with 5% deposit and no LMI if income is under $125,000 single or $200,000 couple.
