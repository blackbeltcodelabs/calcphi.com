How Social Security Benefits Are Calculated

Social Security retirement benefits are calculated using a formula based on your Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME) — the average of your 35 highest-earning years, adjusted for inflation. The formula is progressive: lower earners replace a higher percentage of their income than higher earners.

The 2026 Social Security Bend Point Formula

Your Primary Insurance Amount (PIA) — your benefit at full retirement age — is calculated as: 90% of the first $1,226 of AIME, plus 32% of AIME between $1,226 and $7,391, plus 15% of AIME above $7,391. These "bend points" are adjusted annually for wage inflation.

Example: $75,000 average earnings = $6,250/month AIME. PIA = ($1,226 × 0.90) + (($6,250 − $1,226) × 0.32) = $1,103 + $1,608 = $2,711/month at full retirement age (67).

Waiting until 70 pays 24% more per month than claiming at 67 (full retirement age). If you live past age 80, waiting until 70 produces more lifetime income. If you have health concerns suggesting shorter life expectancy, claiming earlier may maximize lifetime benefits. Married couples should coordinate — typically the higher earner should delay to maximize the survivor benefit.

2026 Social Security Maximum Benefit

The maximum Social Security benefit for someone retiring at full retirement age (67) in 2026 is approximately $4,018/month. Claiming at 70 can produce up to $4,873/month. To receive the maximum, you must have earned at or above the Social Security wage base ($168,600 in 2026) for at least 35 years. Frequently Asked Questions

When can I start collecting Social Security?+
You can begin collecting Social Security retirement benefits as early as age 62, but at a permanent reduction. Full Retirement Age (FRA) is 67 for those born 1960 or later. You can delay up to age 70, earning an additional 8% per year past FRA — the maximum benefit.
Does Social Security income get taxed?+
Up to 85% of Social Security benefits can be subject to federal income tax if your combined income (AGI + non-taxable interest + half of SS benefits) exceeds $25,000 (single) or $32,000 (married). Most states do not tax Social Security benefits.
How many years do I need to work to get Social Security?+
You need 40 credits (roughly 10 years of work) to qualify for retirement benefits. Benefits are calculated using your 35 highest-earning years. Years with no earnings count as $0 — so working beyond 35 years replaces low-earning years with higher ones.
