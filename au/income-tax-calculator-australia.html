Australian Income Tax 2025-26

Australians pay federal income tax based on progressive brackets, plus a 2% Medicare Levy on all income above the low-income threshold. The Stage 3 tax cuts, effective from 1 July 2024, significantly changed the tax brackets and expanded the 19% band. Australia has no state income tax, unlike the US or Canada.

2025-26 Tax Brackets (ATO)

$0 to $18,200: Nil (tax-free threshold). $18,201 to $45,000: 19 cents per dollar. $45,001 to $120,000: $5,092 plus 32.5 cents per dollar over $45,000. $120,001 to $180,000: $29,467 plus 37 cents per dollar over $120,000. Above $180,000: $51,667 plus 45 cents per dollar over $180,000. Plus 2% Medicare Levy on most income.

Key Offsets and Rebates

Low Income Tax Offset (LITO): Up to $700 for incomes under $37,500, reducing to nil at $66,667. Low and Middle Income Tax Offset (LMITO) was abolished from 2022-23. Senior Australians Tax Offset (SATO) for eligible seniors. Franking credits from fully franked dividends reduce tax payable and can result in a refund.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tax-free threshold in Australia 2026?+
The tax-free threshold is $18,200 for Australian residents in 2025-26. You pay no income tax on your first $18,200 of income. This is automatically applied if you claim the tax-free threshold on your Tax File Number declaration with your employer.
What is the Medicare Levy?+
The Medicare Levy is 2% of taxable income, payable by most Australian residents. It funds the public health system. You may receive a Medicare Levy reduction if your income is below certain thresholds, or exemption if you are not entitled to Medicare benefits. The Medicare Levy Surcharge (1-1.5%) applies additionally if you earn above $93,000 and do not have private hospital cover.
How does Australia tax capital gains?+
Capital gains are added to your income and taxed at your marginal rate. However, assets held for more than 12 months receive a 50% CGT discount - only half the gain is included in your assessable income. For a person in the 37% bracket, the effective CGT rate on long-term gains is 18.5%, or 22.5% including Medicare Levy.
