How Australian Superannuation Works

Superannuation is Australia mandatory retirement savings system. Your employer must contribute 11.5% of your ordinary time earnings (OTE) into your super fund from 1 July 2024, rising to 12% on 1 July 2025. These Superannuation Guarantee (SG) contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at your marginal income tax rate, making super highly tax-efficient for most workers.

Contribution Types and Limits 2026

Concessional contributions (employer SG plus voluntary salary sacrifice): $30,000 annual cap. Non-concessional contributions (after-tax): $110,000 annual cap. Concessional contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund (30% for incomes above $250,000 due to Division 293 tax). Non-concessional contributions come from already-taxed money and grow tax-free inside super.

Super Investment Returns

The average balanced super fund has returned approximately 8.5% per year over the past 30 years. The MySuper product from major funds like AustralianSuper, REST, and Hostplus have consistently outperformed the median. Fees matter significantly over a 40-year career - a 0.5% fee difference compounds to approximately $100,000 on a $500,000 final balance.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the super guarantee rate in 2026?+
The Superannuation Guarantee rate is 11.5% of ordinary time earnings from 1 July 2024, increasing to 12% from 1 July 2025. Your employer must pay this into your nominated super fund for any employee earning more than $450 per month (this threshold was abolished in 2022, so all employees are now covered).
How much super should I have at my age?+
Rough benchmarks: $55,000 by 30, $140,000 by 40, $280,000 by 50, $500,000 by 60. The ASFA comfortable retirement standard (2026) requires approximately $690,000 for a single person and $990,000 for a couple. These are ballpark figures - your actual need depends on lifestyle and expected retirement age.
Can I make extra contributions to super?+
Yes. You can make concessional (pre-tax) contributions up to $30,000 total per year including employer SG. Salary sacrifice arrangements reduce your taxable income and are taxed at 15% in super rather than your marginal rate. Non-concessional (after-tax) contributions up to $110,000 per year are also permitted.
