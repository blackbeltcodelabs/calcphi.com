What is Negative Gearing?

Negative gearing occurs when your rental property expenses (mortgage interest, maintenance, rates, insurance, property management, depreciation) exceed your rental income. The resulting net rental loss is deductible against your other income (such as your salary), reducing your total tax payable. Australia is one of few countries to allow this deduction without restriction.

How the Tax Saving Works

If your rental property makes a $15,000 annual loss and you are in the 37% tax bracket plus 2% Medicare Levy, your tax saving is $15,000 times 39% equals $5,850. Your actual after-tax cash loss is only $9,150 rather than $15,000. You are betting that capital appreciation exceeds this ongoing loss plus your equity invested.

Depreciation - The Non-Cash Deduction

Depreciation on building structure (2.5% per year on construction cost) and fixtures reduces taxable rental income without requiring cash outlay. A $600,000 property may have $8,000 to $12,000 of annual depreciation claims. This turns a cash-flow positive property into a negatively geared one on paper, delivering tax benefits while the property generates positive cash flow.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is negative gearing still allowed in Australia 2026?+
Yes. Negative gearing - deducting rental property losses against other income - remains fully allowed in Australia in 2026 despite ongoing political debate about limiting it to new properties. The current government has not changed the rules since the 2016 election campaign when it was a major issue.
What is the CGT discount on investment property?+
Australian investment properties held for more than 12 months receive a 50% CGT discount. Only half the capital gain is included in your assessable income. For someone in the 37% bracket, the effective tax rate on a long-term property gain is 18.5% plus 1% Medicare Levy equals 19.5% effective rate.
Should I negatively gear property?+
Negative gearing makes financial sense only if you believe capital growth will exceed your after-tax losses over the holding period. It does not make sense purely for tax savings - you are losing more cash than you save in tax. The strategy only works with strong capital growth, typically in major city markets.
