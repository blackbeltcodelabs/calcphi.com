NSW Stamp Duty (Transfer Duty) 2026

NSW stamp duty is calculated on a sliding scale based on the property purchase price. First home buyers receive the most generous concessions - full exemption up to $800,000 and a partial concession between $800,000 and $1,000,000. From 2023, first home buyers also have the option of an annual property tax (land tax) instead of paying stamp duty upfront.

NSW Stamp Duty Rates 2026

Properties up to $15,000: $1.25 per $100. $15,001 to $32,000: $187 plus $1.50 per $100 over $15,000. $32,001 to $85,000: $442 plus $1.75 per $100 over $32,000. $85,001 to $319,000: $1,369 plus $3.50 per $100 over $85,000. $319,001 to $1,064,000: $9,559 plus $4.50 per $100 over $319,000. Over $1,064,000: $43,104 plus $5.50 per $100 over $1,064,000.

First Home Buyer Choice - Annual Property Tax

Since January 2023, eligible first home buyers can choose to pay an annual property tax instead of stamp duty upfront. The annual tax for owner-occupiers is $400 plus 0.3% of land value per year. For a property with $600,000 land value, this is approximately $2,200 per year. This breaks even with upfront stamp duty at approximately year 12 to 15. Best for buyers who may sell within 10 years or who are cash-constrained at purchase.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is stamp duty in NSW on $1,000,000?+
On a $1,000,000 property in NSW for a non-first home buyer: $43,104 plus 4.50% on $319,001 to $1,000,000 equals $9,559 plus ($680,999 times 4.5%) equals $43,104 plus $30,644.96 equals approximately $42,204. First home buyers pay a concessional rate between $800,000 and $1,000,000.
Do first home buyers pay stamp duty in NSW?+
First home buyers are fully exempt from stamp duty on properties priced up to $800,000. Between $800,000 and $1,000,000, a concessional duty applies. Above $1,000,000, full duty is payable. The property must be the buyers principal place of residence.
What is the First Home Buyer Annual Property Tax in NSW?+
An alternative to upfront stamp duty where eligible first home buyers pay $400 plus 0.3% of land value per year. This option suits buyers who may sell within 10-12 years since the annual tax becomes more expensive than upfront stamp duty over the long term. The choice must be made at purchase - you cannot change later.
