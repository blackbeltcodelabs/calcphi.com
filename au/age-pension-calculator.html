How the Australian Age Pension Works

The Age Pension is a means-tested government payment available to Australians who have reached pension age (currently 67) and meet income and assets tests. It provides a safety net for retirees with insufficient superannuation. The maximum Age Pension provides approximately $26,800 per year for singles and $40,400 per year for couples combined (2026).

2025-26 Age Pension Rates (Fortnightly)

Single: $1,116.30 per fortnight plus supplements equals approximately $1,144 per fortnight or $29,700 per year. Couples combined: $1,682.80 per fortnight plus supplements equals approximately $1,721 per fortnight or $44,750 per year. These rates are indexed to CPI or PBLCI, whichever is higher, every March and September.

Assets Test Thresholds 2025-26

Homeowners singles: Full pension below $295,500, nil pension above $656,500. Homeowners couples: Full pension below $443,500, nil pension above $986,000. Non-homeowner thresholds are higher by $211,500. The family home is exempt from the assets test. Once assets exceed the full pension threshold, the pension reduces by $3 per fortnight for every $1,000 of assets above the threshold.

Australian Financial Planning Essentials

Australian financial planning centers on the interaction between the compulsory superannuation system, income tax, and government benefit programs. The Superannuation Guarantee of 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025) means most Australian workers are accumulating substantial retirement savings automatically, funded by employer contributions that fall outside their take-home pay. Super contributions are taxed at 15% inside the fund rather than at the marginal income tax rate, making voluntary contributions through salary sacrifice tax-efficient for most workers above the 19% income bracket.

Key Australian Tax Features

The Australian tax system has several distinctive features. Franking credits attached to fully franked dividends from Australian companies eliminate double taxation on company profits and can result in a tax refund for low-income investors. The 50% capital gains tax discount applies to assets held for more than 12 months, halving the effective CGT rate. Negative gearing allows rental property losses to be deducted against other income without restriction. The tax-free threshold of $18,200 means Australian residents pay no income tax on their first $18,200 of income. Medicare at 2% funds universal healthcare. The Medicare Levy Surcharge of 1 to 1.5% applies to higher income earners without private hospital insurance.

2025-26 Key Australian Financial Numbers

Superannuation Guarantee rate: 11.5% (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Annual concessional contribution cap: $30,000. Annual non-concessional cap: $110,000. Transfer balance cap for moving super to pension phase: $1,900,000. Income tax-free threshold: $18,200. HECS-HELP repayment threshold: $54,435. Age Pension eligibility age: 67. Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income. Medicare Levy Surcharge: 1% to 1.5% above $93,000 for singles without private hospital cover. Age Pension full rate single: approximately $1,144 per fortnight. These figures adjust periodically and should be verified at ato.gov.au before making significant decisions. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Age Pension eligibility age in Australia?+
The Age Pension eligibility age is 67 for anyone born on or after 1 January 1957. You must also be an Australian resident for at least 10 years, including at least 5 continuous years, to qualify.
Is the family home included in Age Pension assets test?+
No. Your primary residence (family home) is fully exempt from the assets test regardless of its value. However, a home of exceptional value may be assessed under a separate provision. If you sell your home, the proceeds are assessable as assets for 24 months.
Can I have super and still get Age Pension?+
Yes if your assets and income are below the thresholds. Many retirees receive a part pension on top of their super drawdown. Super in accumulation phase (before retirement) for people under pension age was previously exempt from assets test but is now assessed. APRA-regulated super funds are assessed at their current balance.
